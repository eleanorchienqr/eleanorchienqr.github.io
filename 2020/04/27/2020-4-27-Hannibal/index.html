美剧《汉尼拔》台词精读

نُشر في 2020-04-27 عُدل في 2021-03-11 في Entertainment مشاهدات: Valine:
عدد الحروف في المقال: 3k زمن القراءة ≈ 3 دقائق.

本篇文章是汉尼拔系列某些剧集片段的台词总结，精致古典乐和“晚餐”更相配。

Season02Episode01: Kaiseki

Sentences

  1. These are allegations of misconduct. 这些是对管理不善的指控。
  2. In my opinion, it was a lapse in judgment. 判断失误。
  3. A federal examiner is someone who arrives at a battlefield after the battle and bayonets the wounded. 联邦调查员在战斗结束后下场处理伤员的人。
  4. There is a general desire to see this go away, quickly and quietly. 大家都希望这件事就这么算了。
  5. In light of that, I would greatly appreciate it if you could recant your report. 正因为如此，我希望你可以撤回你的报告。
  6. She is not easily swayed. 她并不容易被动摇。
  7. Will Graham asking to see you betrays his clear intent to manipulate you. 暴露了想要控制你的意图。
  8. He sees his own mentality as grotesque but useful. 精神状态异于常人但颇有益处。
  9. I imagine there’s an honesty in that you can relate to. 我认为这种坦荡让你找到了共鸣。
  10. I used to hear my thoughts inside my skull with the same tone, timber, accent, as if the words were coming out of my mouth. 过去我总能听见相同的声调、音色和口音在我脑子里说出我的想法，就好像那些词语是我亲口说的。
  11. Friendship can sometimes involve a breach of individual separateness. 友情有时会打破人与人之间的个体独立性。
  12. Giving your thoughts words encourage clarity. 让想法发出声音可以看清事实。
  13. Lessons learned from cellular decay: enjoy the world while we have it and give it a little bit back. 细胞腐败告诉我们：趁现在享受生活而且要有所回馈。
  14. When possible, I try to leave an indelible mark wherever I go. 留下不可磨灭的痕迹。
  15. You maintain an air of transparency while putting me in the position to lie for you. 你总是一副坦然的样子，却把我置于为你说谎的境地。
  16. Automatism allows a defendant to argue they shouldn’t be held criminally liable for their actions due to unconsciousness. 自动症允许被告抗辩他们不应该为无意识行为负刑事责任。
  17. It’s sad to see a brilliant psychiatrist fall for such hoary old chestnuts. 看着一位优秀的心理医生被如此陈旧的把戏所戏弄，真是可惜。
  18. Shot them up with a little China White, injected them with preservatives, then filled the bodies with silicone so they don’t emaciate, then he seals them with a hard resin shell. 注射了高浓度海洛因和防腐剂，然后注满硅酮防止塌缩，最后封在硬树脂壳里。
  19. Declaring Hannibal’s guilt is more important to Will than establishing his own defense. 宣布汉尼拔有罪比自己的辩护更重要。
  20. There wasn’t an orgy of evidence. 没有留下多少证据。

Phrases

  • lapse |læps| noun. a short period of time during which you fail to do something well or properly, often caused by not being careful. 疏忽，失误:
    • momentary / temporary / occasional etc lapse
    • [+ in ] lapses in security. 保安方面的疏忽
  • bayonet |ˈbeənɪt, ˈbeɪənət|
    • noun. a long knife that is fixed to the end of a rifle (=long gun)〔步枪上的〕刺刀，枪刺
    • verb. to push the point of a bayonet into someone 用刺刀刺
  • recant |rɪˈkænt, rɪˈkænt| verb [I,T][formal] to say publicly that you no longer have a political or religious belief that you had before. 公开宣布放弃（以前的政治观点或宗教信仰）
  • grotesque |groˈtɛsk, grəʊˈtesk| adj.
    • unpleasant, shocking, and offensive 怪诞的，荒唐的: It’s grotesque to portray peace campaigners as unpatriotic. 把和平运动的倡导者说成是不爱国，这很荒唐。
    • extremely ugly in a strange or unnatural way 极丑陋的，怪异的: a grotesque figure with a huge head. 长着硕大脑袋的丑陋身影
  • indelible |ɪnˈdɛləbl, ɪnˈdeləbəl| adj.
    • impossible to remove or forget 难以去掉的; 难以忘记的; 永恒的.【SYN】→permanent
    • indelible ink / pencil / marker etc, ink etc that makes a permanent mark which cannot be removed 不褪色墨水/铅笔/记号笔等
  • liable |ˈlaɪəbl, ˈlaɪəbəl| adj. [not before noun] liable to do sth, likely to do or say something or to behave in a particular way, especially because of a fault or natural tendency. 可能（易于）做某事;【SYN】→likely
  • orgy |ˈɔrdʒɪ, ˈɔ:dʒi| noun. ((plural) orgies)[C]
    • a wild party with a lot of eating, drinking, and sexual activity.〔纵欲的〕狂欢宴会，纵酒宴乐
    • an orgy of sth, used to emphasize that people suddenly do a lot of a particular activity, especially far too much of it. 放纵，无节制:
      • an orgy of spending 大肆挥霍
      • an orgy of violence 滥用暴力
